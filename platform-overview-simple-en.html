BulanVarMı?

How the lost & found platform works and what people can do on it — explained without any technical or coding detail.

BulanVarMı? is a website where people who lost something and people who found something can find each other. A person who lost an item posts about it, a person who found an item posts about it too, and the platform helps connect the two sides so the item can make its way back to its rightful owner.

How It Works

01
Something gets lost or found
A person loses a wallet, phone, keys, bag, pet, or any other item — or someone else finds something that isn't theirs.
02
They post a listing
A short description, a photo, and where and when it happened. It's marked either "Lost" or "Found."
03
The platform looks for matches
Lost and Found listings are compared automatically, and likely matches are suggested to both sides — no manual searching required.
04
The two people connect and the item is returned
The person claims the item, proves it's theirs with a few details, chats with the other side, and arranges to get it back. The listing is then marked resolved.

What People Can Do

Lost flow

Report a Lost Item

Describe what was lost, where, and when, and add a photo. The platform can help suggest a description or work out the category from the photo.

Found flow

Report a Found Item

Same idea, but for something someone else lost — helping the true owner recognise and find it.

General

Search and Browse

Look through all listings, filter by type, category, date, or location, and sort them however is most useful.

General

Browse on a Map

See lost and found items pinned on a map, to search visually by area instead of reading through lists.

Found flow

Claim an Item

If a found item looks like theirs, someone can claim it by describing details only the real owner would know. The listing owner reviews and approves or declines.

Lost flow

Report a Sighting

Someone who spotted a lost item somewhere, without picking it up, can leave a note about where they saw it.

General

Chat Privately

Once a claim is approved, the two people get a private chat to arrange the handover — no personal contact details need to be shared upfront.

General

Save Favourites

Keep an eye on listings that might be relevant, without committing to a claim yet.

Lost flow

Set Up Alerts

Save a search — like "lost wallets in Kadıköy" — and get notified automatically the moment a matching listing appears.

Lost flow

Print a Poster

Turn a listing into a printable flyer to put up around the neighbourhood.

General

Rate Each Other

After an item is returned, both sides can leave a star rating, building trust for future interactions on the platform.

General

Business Accounts

Hotels, malls, courier companies, schools and similar organisations that regularly handle lost items can switch to a business profile with tools to manage many listings at once.

Trust and Safety

Listings are reviewed

Every new listing is checked before it becomes publicly visible, keeping out spam or inappropriate content.

Reporting abuse

Any listing or profile can be reported for being misleading, spammy, or inappropriate. Admins review every report.

Private by default

Contact details aren't shared directly — people connect only after a claim is approved, and only through the in-platform chat.

Reputation system

Ratings, points, and badges build a visible track record, so people can judge trustworthiness before dealing with someone.

In short

The platform's whole purpose is to shorten the distance between "I lost something" and "someone found it" — through easy reporting, automatic matching suggestions, a safe way to prove ownership, and a private way to arrange the return.
